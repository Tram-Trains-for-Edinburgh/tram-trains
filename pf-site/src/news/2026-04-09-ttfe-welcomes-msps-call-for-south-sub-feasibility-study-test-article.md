title: TTfE WELCOMES MSP'S CALL FOR SOUTH SUB FEASIBILITY STUDY - TEST ARTICLE
date: 2026-04-09T08:20:00.000+01:00
---
Tram Trains for Edinburgh has welcomed comments made this week by  MSP (Edinburgh Southern) calling on Transport Scotland to commission a formal feasibility study into restoring passenger services on the South Suburban Railway.

Speaking at Holyrood, Mr Johnson described the South Sub corridor as "one of the most significant untapped transport assets in Scotland," noting that communities including Craigmillar, Portobello, and Morningside remain poorly served by the current public transport network despite sitting within minutes of the existing tram route.

Rob Falcon, Chair of Tram Trains for Edinburgh, said: "We're delighted to see Daniel raising this issue at the highest level. A feasibility study is exactly the right next step — it would give transport planners the evidence base they need to move forward with confidence."

The campaign, which launched in late 2024, advocates for tram-train technology as the most practical and cost-effective means of restoring services on the freight-only line, which last carried passengers in 1962. Tram trains are capable of running on both heavy rail infrastructure and existing tram tracks, meaning services could connect seamlessly with Edinburgh's tram network at points including Brunstane and Cameron Toll.

TTfE will be presenting to the public at King's Buildings on 23 April. All are welcome.
